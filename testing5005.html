Henar Press

We are a nonprofit, independent publisher based in Columbus, Ohio, dedicated to translating and publishing experimental Kurdish literature.

We believe in the power of literature in the face of ongoing ethnic cleansing. Through our writing, we seek to remember, to resist, and to reclaim our stories. As children of genocide and displacement, we honor the memories of the Halabja massacre, the Anfal campaign, and the countless Kurds persecuted for their courage to write.

But we are not bound by our trauma. Nor are we afraid to confront our paradoxes. We proudly publish writing that complicates the supposed Kurdish question: pacifist and militant, nationalistic and secular, stateless and rooted.

We celebrate the avant-garde and the unafraid. Our books blur the lines between poetry and prose, history and fiction, personal and political. We are a haven for Kurdish writers who refuse to be silenced and bound by convention.

In our own small way, Henar Press is a rebellion against the erasure of our identity.

Contribute

As a nonprofit, we rely on the generosity of our supporters. Every contribution, no matter how small, makes a big impact on our mission. We face significant financial challenges as a small team, and your support helps us bring Kurdish voices to a wider audience.

Donate

There are also other ways to support us. You can buy our books or request them at your local library or bookstores. If you're interested in volunteering, please contact us. To stay updated on events and news from Henar Press, sign up for our newsletter and follow our social media accounts.

If you're a Kurdish writer looking to submit your work, visit our contact page to learn about our submission process. We welcome prose and poetry in any language. Additionally, if you're interested in reading more Kurdish literature, we're building a comprehensive directory of Kurdish literature available in English.
