Our Story

We're a nonprofit, independent publisher based in Columbus, Ohio, dedicated to translating and publishing the best of Kurdish literature. We're excited to share with English readers what we believe is one of the literary world's best kept secrets.


Like many Kurds in the diaspora, my life has been shaped by displacement and exile. My parents fled Iraqi Kurdistan during Saddam Hussein's Anfal campaign, seeking asylum in Norway. I spent my childhood moving between continents, never truly finding a permanent home.

As I grew older and began reading more deeply, I searched for novels that reflected my identity, but they were hard to find. I had to wait until 2016 to read the first Kurdish novel available in English, Bakhtiyar Ali's I Stared at the Night of the City, translated by Kareem Abdulrahman.

Reading about Sulaymaniyah was a profoundly moving experience. Great fiction has the ability to change your life, and this book did exactly that.

I read all the Kurdish literature I could find. Jalal Barzanji's The Man in Blue Pyjamas, Choman Hardi's Life for Us, Ava Homa's Daughters of Smoke and Fire, among many more incredible works. But my excitement was short-lived. Within months, I had exhausted the entire catalog of Kurdish literature available in English.

So, my wife and I founded Henar Press to fill this gap. Our mission is to translate and publish Kurdish literature for a global audience. We're excited to share the stories and experiences that have shaped our community.

What does Henar mean?

Henar is the Kurdish word for pomegranate. It's the national symbol of Kurdistan and has been a recurring motif in Kurdish literature since Mem û Zîn. We were personally drawn to the pomegranate tree's ability to flourish in the harshest conditions.

Who are the Kurds?

The Kurds are an indigenous people spanning Turkey, Iraq, Iran, and Syria. For centuries, we've faced genocide and colonization, from the Ottoman Empire's forced assimilation to the Ba'athist ethnic cleansing in Iraq. Despite efforts to erase our identity, we've maintained a strong determination to preserve our languages, traditions, and culture.

What does Against Forgetting, We Write mean?

We write to remember, to resist, and to reclaim our stories. Our words honor the memories of the Halabja massacre, the Anfal campaign, and the countless Kurdish writers imprisoned and killed for their work.

Do all Kurds speak the same language?

While Kurdish is often referred to as a single language with multiple dialects, it's more accurately a family of distinct languages. For example, a Sorani speaker wouldn't understand Kurmanji, and vice versa.
